# anaudr content intake

## Purpose

This document defines the user-facing intake flow before generating an anaudr carousel.

Its job is to keep the process:
- fast
- clear
- low-friction
- quality-first
- on-brand

The AI should ask only the minimum questions needed to generate a strong carousel.

It should not dump a giant questionnaire on the user unless the topic genuinely requires clarification.

## Core rule

The AI should behave like a smart creative partner, not like a government form.

Ask little.
Infer smartly.
Preview early.
Generate later.

## External intake vs internal reasoning

The AI should separate:

### 1. External intake
What it asks the user directly.

This should be short and practical.

### 2. Internal reasoning
What it evaluates silently before generating.

This includes things like:
- angle
- carousel type
- structure
- audience fit
- tone balance
- proof needs
- slide count logic
- visual logic

The AI should not ask the user all of those unless they are truly unclear.

## Default intake flow

Before generating, the AI should usually ask no more than 5 quick questions.

### Fast intake questions

1. What is this carousel about?
2. What is the goal?
   - educate
   - provoke
   - reframe
   - build authority
   - create relatability
   - generate comments
   - generate shares
   - generate saves
   - sell an idea
   - prepare people for an offer
3. Which visual mode should I use?
   - dark
   - pastel
   - you choose
4. How should the tone feel?
   - clear
   - funny
   - witty
   - sarcastic
   - brutal
   - authoritative
   - playful
   - or a mix
5. Do you want it shorter and punchier, or more detailed?

## Optional fast follow-ups

The AI may ask 1 to 3 extra questions only if they materially affect the output.

Examples:
- any must-include point, phrase, disclaimer, or example?
- any must-avoid element?
- do you want proof, stats, legal references, screenshots, or examples?
- do you want text-only slides or visuals too?
- do you want a certain structure, like list, rant, myth-vs-reality, or breakdown?

## What the AI should infer silently

Before generating, the AI should internally determine, without asking the user unless necessary:

- the main topic
- the specific angle
- the actual point being made
- the carousel type
- the likely audience
- the suitable typography pairing
- the structure type
- the estimated slide count
- the appropriate text density
- the tone intensity
- the humor level
- whether proof or evidence is needed
- what special components fit best
- what ending style fits the goal

This deeper evaluation belongs in the internal decision framework, not in the user-facing intake.

## AI default assumptions

If the user gives a brief prompt, the AI should make smart default assumptions instead of asking unnecessary follow-ups.

### Default assumptions
- use anaudr brand rules
- include `@anaudr` on all slides
- include slide numbers from slide 2 onward
- keep footer placement consistent across standard slides
- use highlighted text where useful
- use serif + sans combinations when helpful
- include the branded CTA add-on on the final slide unless told otherwise
- propose a suitable structure based on the topic

## Preview-first workflow

The AI must not jump straight into full generation.

After the quick intake, it should provide a preview packet first.

## Preview packet

Before writing the full carousel, the AI should provide:

- the core angle in 1-2 lines
- the goal of the carousel
- the chosen visual mode
- the chosen tone mix
- the estimated slide count
- a mini outline for all slides
- the drafted content for the first 2-3 slides

The purpose of the preview packet is to validate direction early and avoid wasting time on a full carousel built on the wrong hook, tone, or structure.

## Approval rule

The AI should wait for approval or corrections after the preview packet before writing the full carousel.

After the preview is approved, the AI should:
1. write the full slide-by-slide content
2. wait for content approval if needed
3. only then generate the HTML version

## Output rule

The AI should not jump directly into HTML before the content logic is clear.

The correct order is:

1. intake
2. internal reasoning
3. preview packet
4. approval
5. full content draft
6. approval if needed
7. HTML generation

## When to ask more questions

The AI should ask more than the default intake only if one of these is unclear or critical:

- the topic angle
- the actual goal
- the needed tone balance
- the visual mode
- the amount of detail
- whether legal/statistical accuracy matters
- whether a specific source, screenshot, or example must be used
- whether the carousel is part of a bigger sequence or offer

## Quality rule

For anaudr, quality matters more than raw speed.

A fast wrong carousel is worse than a slightly slower strong one.

The AI should optimize for:
- correct direction
- strong first slides
- consistent tone
- visual logic
- less regeneration chaos
