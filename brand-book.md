# anaudr brand book

## Purpose

This document defines the anaudr brand identity for Instagram carousels and related visual content.

Its role is to make sure the brand feels:
- recognizable
- coherent
- premium
- expressive
- smart
- readable
- unmistakably anaudr

This file defines **identity**:
- tone
- personality
- visual DNA
- palette
- typography
- emoji behavior
- footer identity

This file is **not** the place for workflow or detailed generation logic.

---

## Brand objective

anaudr should feel like a brand made by:
- a very smart woman
- with taste
- with legal and business literacy
- with internet literacy
- with a sharp bullshit detector
- with humor
- with design instinct
- with zero patience for dead content

The brand should feel:
- bold
- strategic
- premium
- witty
- grounded
- easy to recognize
- emotionally clear
- visually alive

- sarcasm
- wit

- sharpness
- detail
- realism

- like a very smart woman explaining serious things without sounding boring

4. real detail, nuance, proof, or examples
5. a punchy ending line that sounds human

## Color behavior

Color must create:
- hierarchy
- clarity
- mood
- rhythm

Color must not create:
- noise
- confusion
- decorative chaos

### Core color rules
- each carousel should use one dominant theme
- each slide should have clear accent hierarchy
- accent colors are tools, not confetti
- not every phrase deserves a highlight
- dark theme may use stronger contrast
- color theme may use softer contrast, but still needs authority

### Background rotation rule
For `COLOR_THEME`, backgrounds may rotate:
- Slide 1 = Butter Cream
- Slide 2 = Baby Blue
- Slide 3 = Lavender
- Slide 4 = Peach
- then repeat as needed

This rotation is allowed, not mandatory.

## Typography

### Official font pairings

#### For legal, business, bold opinion, authority-heavy content
- Headline: Playfair Display
- Body: DM Sans

#### For psychology, self-development, relationship, and softer commentary content
- Headline: Cormorant Garamond
- Body: Work Sans

### Typography behavior
- serif is used for authority, drama, elegance, emotional or editorial weight
- sans-serif is used for explanation, labels, lists, numbers, footer, metadata, and UI logic
- serif and sans-serif can and should be mixed on the same slide when hierarchy benefits from it
- the same slide may contain:
  - display serif headline
  - sans labels
  - sans explanation
  - highlighted punch phrase

### Typography mood
Headlines should feel:
- bold
- editorial
- distinctive
- branded
- scroll-stopping

Body text should feel:
- highly readable
- intelligently structured
- confident
- not tiny
- not timid

### Text hierarchy
Typical hierarchy:
- Hero hook: display font, approx. `100–120px`, bold/black
- Section headline: display font, approx. `56–84px`
- Subheading / label: body font, approx. `34–38px`, semibold
- Body / explanation: body font, approx. `30–36px`
- Handle / footer metadata: body font, approx. `22–24px`
- Slide number: sans-serif only

These are directional, not mechanical.

## Highlight language

Highlighting is part of the brand signature.

Allowed highlight styles:
- flat yellow highlight
- mustard / warm yellow highlight
- salmon highlight
- coral highlight
- underlined phrase
- accent-colored word
- chunky highlighted phrase block
- gradient word treatment when elegant

Use highlights for:
- key phrases
- sarcasm or punch
- legal/business keywords
- contrast points
- ironic emphasis
- takeaways
- CTA phrases

Do not highlight everything.
If everything screams, nothing lands.

---

## Spacing and framing

The brand should feel framed, not squeezed.

### Canvas
- `1080x1080` or `1080x1350`

### General spacing
- outer margin usually `60–80px`
- vertical gap between major sections usually `40–60px`
- spacing between headline and body usually `20–30px`
- layouts should feel balanced and intentional

### Top safety space
Every slide must respect a **clear top safety zone**.

Important text, labels, and intro elements must never feel glued to the top edge.

The slide should breathe from above.

## Emoji system

### Emoji policy
Emojis are part of the brand language.

They may be used as:
- inline accents
- list markers
- mood setters
- section cues
- callout enhancers
- oversized transparent background elements
- stickers or visual anchors when appropriate

### Emoji behavior
Emojis should:
- support hierarchy
- support scanning
- support tone
- support humor

Emojis should not:
- replace hierarchy
- create clutter
- become childish decoration

### Emoji frequency
There is no fixed maximum per slide.

Use as many as needed **if they genuinely help**.

### Oversized emoji rule
Oversized background emojis are allowed if:
- low opacity
- clearly integrated
- they do not harm readability

---

## Footer identity

### Handle rule
`@anaudr` must appear on every slide.

### Handle styling
- sans-serif only
- around `22–24px`
- regular or light weight
- clearly visible
- visually quiet

### Slide number rule
Slide numbers are mandatory from slide 2 to the final slide unless intentionally waived.

### Slide number styling
- sans-serif only
- clear but not dominant
- stable placement
- aligned with the footer system

### Footer divider
A footer separator is allowed and encouraged when it improves structure.

Possible separators:
- dashed line
- fine solid line
- subtle divider band
- low-contrast separator

The footer should feel:
- quiet
- organized
- branded
- consistent
